# on public criticism of "kill gameplay"

## not a manifesto

i will not immediately attempt to explain what "kill gameplay" means, and it is very possible that even when i am satisfied with the project, there will be a notable lack of a clear statement or summary of any sort. while "kill gameplay" is grammatically an imperative statement, this repository will not make any additional imperatives, nor rephrase the existing imperative.

i will also not seek to gather a body of statements that i have made in the past in order to explain or describe what "kill gameplay" means, except as a component ancillary to the following:

## 1; a body of public criticism

within this repository i'd like to gather a body of other public responses, reactions, and observations regarding "kill gameplay." the term 'criticism' may not necessarily apply to all items, but for my purposes i will focus mainly on negative or critical responses, reactions, and observations; the use of the term 'criticism' has thus been selected to fulfill the role of a focusing lens, rather than as a descriptive term.

## 2; counter-criticism

separate from the 'body of public criticism,' in such a way that the original criticism itself can stand and be observed alone as a mass without my interference, i will attempt to record all counter-criticism that i have found meaningful -- criticism again, as defined above in section 1, not necessarily meaning only negative/disagreement reactions, but also including, for example, neutral and/or agreeable observations.

## 3; a collection of statements to move the discourse forward

in section 1, i will collect a body of 'public criticism,' ideally unedited but organized in such a way as to make it more apprehendable (for example, compacting multiple instances of the same statement into one place, or including only a small representative group if there are a very large amount of nearly identical statements without meaningful variation)

in section 2, i will present counter-criticism to the body produced in section 1

in this final section, section 3, i will attempt to synthesize both previous sections into a usable summary, whose purpose is to facilitate future discussion by avoiding re-treading discussions that have already occurred.

## inspirations for this process

- [Starseed Observatory](http://starseedobservatory.com), "a mixed-media video games criticism project that critiques the indie video game Starseed Pilgrim and presents the ongoing discourse around the game." it collects a number of articles and comments regarding Starseed Pilgrim, a game i designed. i haven't seen any other attempts before or since to catalogue the "discourse around" any game.

- [Ugly Feelings](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674024090) (Ngai, Sianne), [The Impossible Reversal](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517916220/the-impossible-reversal/) (McDonald, Peter), and [Run and Jump: The Meaning of the 2D Platformer](https://direct.mit.edu/books/oa-monograph/5723/Run-and-JumpThe-Meaning-of-the-2D-Platformer) (McDonald, Peter) address artworks in a particular way that i will describe as _meaning-making_ and _objective,_ terms i will describe below
	- **meaning-making.** these books use art as a source for making meaning, observing and examining them as part of a larger process that moves beyond the pieces themselves. by contrast, critical and constructive commentary tends to focus on the virtues and failures of the works.
	- **objective.** not in the sense that the conclusions are objective statements, but that the conclusions are connected to objective observations regarding the object -- in other words, the books do not say, "i had this experience with the object and here is an interpretation of that experience," but rather, "here is a set of facts about the object and here is an interpretation of those facts." when personal experiences are included, they are explicitly presented in this frame: as the best possible way to describe internal qualia necessary to draw the conclusion.
