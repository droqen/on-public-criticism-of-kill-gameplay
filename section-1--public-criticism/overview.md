# section 1; a body of public criticism

## interactivity ("gameplay") is the only component of games that differentiate them from non-games / if you're not using this component you're either making a non-game or something that shouldn't be a game / if you're not using this component you're not using the unique strength of the medium

> . . . You can't remove interactivity from the medium, by definition. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgzpndI-D6jXbbsLX1J4AaABAg)

> . . . you kind of have to include some basic mechanics (or gameplay, if you will) for it to be a game. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgzjOmHhwvfO17RPkqd4AaABAg)

> If you don't need gameplay to push the story... pick a different medium to tell it. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugx0wCYL8x4nimkl8eF4AaABAg)

> . . . the guy completely missed the point of what a Video Game is able to do . . . [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgzqEFCOVevMuNJFjDd4AaABAg)

> . . . for gaames in particular though the thought experiment of "What is [X] without [defining feature]" is a little less interesting because we Have media without game play; they're called movies. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgyDrvoZ0NzIGFuLE-h4AaABAg)

### not only interactivity but _challenge_ is the key to what games are and can do

> Tuning challenge to best convey what the game wants to achieve IS game design, and as such there is no correct answer to what gets included or excluded, or what challenge level is appropriate without the context of a particular design goal. There are very many games that feature characters sleeping but very few ask you, the player, to stare at a black screen for 8 real life hours to accurately simulate this. The simulation is the context, the gameplay is how you are able to engage with it. Gameplay, aka, interactivity IS the art form. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgyjMbcCxRbYYa5PCjx4AaABAg)

## we already have games without gameplay! (usually citing IF, sandbox games)

> You want games without failure states or challenges? Interactive fiction, visual novels, and sandbox games are thriving [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgyjMbcCxRbYYa5PCjx4AaABAg)

## kill gameplay is presented objective but it's actually subjective / just a matter of opinion

> . . . Droqen and the people behind Dogme 95 have the opinion that there is a correct way to create something, and a wrong way, and the way THEY like things is objectively correct. // This is . . . objectively incorrect. . . . If you look at something and you don't like it, feel free to critique it.  But don't look at things you don't like and begin to believe that your viewpoint is the only correct viewpoint to have. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugx5ey2U0FbJC4eU5MB4AaABAg)

> . . . its clear that what or how people may see in games changes from person to person. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugw2M4i0ZGKVHwbAXjV4AaABAg)

> I think as soon as you've begun an argument with "Art should / should be...", you've already lost. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugztle4ev7Iuscu0kD14AaABAg&pp=0gcJCSIANpG00pGi)

## it's wrong to apply kill gameplay to all games

> . . . the notion that most, or all, video games are wrong for being built the way they are is completely idiotic. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugx5ey2U0FbJC4eU5MB4AaABAg)

## games don't have to have an instrumental function; games can just be fun

## games satisfy a desire to feel a sense of accomplishment

> I play for the gameplay, for the challenge, for the adrenaline spikes. . . I want to feel challenged, i want to feel like what im doing has meaning. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugw2M4i0ZGKVHwbAXjV4AaABAg)

> . . . the fourth step of Maslow's pyramid - "Prestige, legacy", more known as "Sense of achievement". The same sense that pushes us to do things apart from eating or conversing is also the reason we play "sudoku". We want to do something. We want to "have fun". . . this necessity is one thing. Painting and art in general is the fifth step - "Art and self-realisation", a whole different thing, yet of same importance. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=UgwVMeCYe9fkzcExTdZ4AaABAg)

**NOTE:** This one specifically states that the sense of accomplishment is separate from the sense of expression/artistry and positions them relative to one another.

## droqen's position is that games and art must have an instrumental function

> they seem to have philosophized themselves into a position of thinking things must be "useful" to not be evil or bad, and that playing games or engaging with entertainment is not useful, but somehow art itself, as long as it is conveying a message, is useful and thus should exist only to serve that function. [@](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qmt9LX8pTI&lc=Ugztle4ev7Iuscu0kD14AaABAg&pp=0gcJCSIANpG00pGi)
